HISTORY

We made it!

Our start was not very interesting, but through the years of work we had a lot of exciting periods. We started as a small company in 1950 and in just a few years we started working on large UAV aircraft for military purposes. The first aircraft was built in 1956 and is the forerunner of today's drones. Until the 2000s, we worked exclusively with UAVs, but with the advancement of technology, the direction of the company's work changed and already in 2002, the drone was under construction very similar to today's. In just the next few years, the improvements have been huge and the latest model can in no way be compared to the original one.

We are market leaders because our drones are specially tailored for a variety of uses. Although we started with drones exclusively for military use, today we have only 20% of production for that purpose. The best-selling ones are for personal use, most often for photographing and filming space, but we cannot ignore the high carrying capacity and durability of aircraft and their use in rescue missions or simply in ordinary deliveries. We write history, but you are responsible for that!

Frequently Asked Questions

i

Do I need a permit to fly a drone?

In most country a so-called Ascent Permit is required for commercial use of unmanned microcopters (drone). This is issued either individually or generally by the respective states and is valid for several years. We have compiled all the necessary information and local contacts for our customers to easily obtain the permit, and will be provided to you after purchasing a Height-Tech multicopter.

i

How much can a drone carry?

The net load of our strongest drone is currently up to 2,400 g. This includes the weight of the respective camera mount. We generally recommend keeping the net load as low as possible to be able to reach long flight times when needed. For example, current system cameras can achieve close to the video quality of full format cameras, even surpassing them, with only 50% of its weight.

i

How high and far can drones fly?

The range of the drone control is about 1.5 km. Export systems can achieve a range of up to 50 km. In the field our larger drones can therefore operate at a perimeter and altitude of about 200m. When using the drone in the mountains the limits are set by the low atmospheric pressure. However, our drones have already been successfully used at 3,950 m above sea level.

i

Can drones also be used indoors?

The emission-free and silent electric drives of course also allow our drones to be used indoors. Applications are e.g. demo flights at trade fairs or film shots inside buildings. Due to the typically limited space a suitable launch and landing site not accessible by onlookers must be provided. Since the GPS and compass sensor technology limited inside buildings, the pilot must be able to control the drone manually.

i

Which drive technology use?

Our drones are propelled by electricity. We use state-of-the-art external rotor motors which change their speed several hundred times a second. Using external rotor motors allows for propellers to be directly propelled without a loud and heavy drive. These propellers with low speeds are required for the high efficiency. We use the latest lithium polymer battery technology to guarantee the best possible power.

i

What if drone crashes?

After sending the drone to use we will review the data of the flight data recorder. We will simultaneously create a damage analysis. The damage will then be repaired as quickly as possible. Since we typically have all spare parts in stock, your drone will usually be returned within a few days. If you ordered the “First Class Package”, you will promptly receive a replacement drone free of charge.
